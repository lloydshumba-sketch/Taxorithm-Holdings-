Analytics process

A structured approach from raw data to insight.

Effective analytics requires more than importing data into a chart. It requires understanding the client’s business, cleaning the data, defining meaningful measures and presenting results in a way that supports action.

1. Understand the business question

We identify what management needs to know: performance, cash flow, tax exposure, fraud risk, operational efficiency, sales trends or reporting weaknesses.

2. Clean, structure and validate the data

We review data sources, clean records, standardise formats, identify gaps and prepare the information for meaningful analysis.

3. Build dashboards and reporting outputs

We create practical outputs such as dashboards, summaries, exception reports, KPI packs and insight notes that management can use.

Before we start

Information needed for an analytics engagement.

The quality of analytics depends on the quality and availability of source records. We help structure the data, but clients should provide the most complete records available.

Source data

Excel files, accounting exports, sales records, bank statements, payroll records, tax schedules, invoices, supplier lists, customer lists or system reports.

Business question

State what you want to understand: profitability, revenue trends, expense control, overdue debtors, tax exposure, fraud risk, stock movement or performance monitoring.
