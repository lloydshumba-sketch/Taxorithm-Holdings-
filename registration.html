Why formalise

Formal registration creates a stronger foundation for growth.

A registered business is better positioned to approach clients, open formal relationships, maintain tax discipline, build systems and present itself professionally to the market.

01

Professional credibility

Registration, corporate emails and website presence help clients, suppliers and institutions take the business more seriously from the first interaction.

02

Compliance discipline

Formal setup supports clearer tax responsibilities, proper accounting records, statutory follow-up and a more disciplined operating structure.

03

Growth readiness

A formal business structure is easier to scale, finance, document, audit, analyse and present to corporate clients, funders and strategic partners.

Registration process

A structured route from enquiry to business launch.

The process is designed to reduce confusion for clients by identifying what information is needed, what statutory steps are relevant and what additional launch support can strengthen the business.

1. Business information review

We clarify the proposed business activity, preferred name, ownership structure, directors, shareholders, contact details and the intended operating model.

2. Registration and statutory direction

We assist with registration-related information and guide the client on ZIMRA, NSSA and compliance matters that may arise after formal setup.

3. Launch-readiness support

We support corporate emails, website presence, service messaging, basic records setup and practical business infrastructure required for a professional launch.

Prepare before enquiry

Information that helps us process your registration enquiry properly.

Providing complete information at the beginning makes the registration and launch process faster, clearer and more professional.

Names

Proposed business names

Prepare your preferred business names, trading name ideas and a short description of what the business will do.

People

Directors and shareholders

Prepare details of directors, shareholders, ownership arrangements, contact persons and decision-makers involved in the business.

Activity

Business activity

Explain the main services or products, target clients, expected operations and whether the business will employ staff.

Launch

Launch support required

State whether you also need ZIMRA guidance, NSSA guidance, corporate emails, website, branding, bookkeeping setup or tax support.

After registration

What happens after the company is registered?

The most important mistake many new businesses make is treating registration as the end of the process. After registration, the business should immediately organise compliance, records, communication and client-facing systems.

Set up compliance responsibilities

Identify tax registration needs, statutory obligations, filing requirements, payroll-related responsibilities and record-keeping expectations.

Build financial record discipline

Organise invoices, receipts, bank records, sales schedules, expense files, payroll documents and bookkeeping procedures from the start.

Present the business professionally

Use corporate emails, a proper website, clear service messages, consistent branding and official contact channels to build client confidence.
